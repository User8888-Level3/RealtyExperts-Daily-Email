A "Soft" Spring Start:
The early 2026 spring market in Silicon Valley is kicking off slower than expected. Recent data reveals that closed single-family home sales in Santa Clara County dropped 16% year-over-year in January, and the median price softened slightly (down about 3.3% to $1.74M). Months of inventory effectively doubled from December to January, sitting at 1.9 months. It's not a crash, but buyers are definitely pushing back against the "automatic spring surge."
Across Alameda County, the median sale price is sitting right around $925,000 to $1M. The big story here is inventory: active listings have increased significantly compared to this time last year. This is giving buyers more room to breathe and negotiate. While the sale-to-list ratio remains strong (around 102% to 104%), homes that need work or are priced too ambitiously are sitting longer.
